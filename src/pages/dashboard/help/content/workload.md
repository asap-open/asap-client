# Workload Volume & Safety

[Back to Home](/dashboard/help)

ASAP tracks the sheer physical demand you place on your body by measuring **Volume** (Total Weight Lifted × Reps). We use advanced sports-science metrics to keep you progressing safely.

## ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio)
This is the gold standard metric for injury prevention. It compares your immediate fatigue to your historical fitness level.

- **Acute Load:** Your total volume over the **last 7 days**.
- **Chronic Load:** Your average weekly volume over the **previous 4 weeks**.
- **The Ratio:** `Acute Load / Chronic Load`

**How to read your ACWR Status:**
*   **Optimal (0.8 – 1.3):** You are in the "Sweet Spot." You're training hard enough to progress but not so hard that you're at high risk of injury.
*   **High (1.3 – 1.5):** Your load is elevated. Pay close attention to recovery and monitor fatigue signals.
*   **Danger (> 1.5):** You have spiked your training volume too quickly compared to your baseline. You are at high risk of injury and should strongly consider tapering.
*   **Under (< 0.8):** You are training below your baseline. If continued long-term, this leads to detraining.

## Ramp Rate
While ACWR looks at your 4-week baseline, the Ramp Rate measures your immediate progression speed.
- **Calculation:** `Current 7-Day Volume - Previous 7-Day Volume`
- **What it means:** It tells you exactly how many kilograms of volume you have added (or lost) compared to just last week, highlighting sudden jumps in intensity.
